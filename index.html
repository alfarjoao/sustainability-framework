Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using the carbon calculator

What is embodied carbon?

Embodied carbon refers to the total greenhouse gas emissions generated during the extraction, manufacturing, transportation, and construction of building materials and components. This "upfront" carbon represents approximately 11% of global CO₂ emissions and is critical in renovation versus demolition decisions because it accounts for all the carbon already "locked" into existing structures.

What is operational carbon?

Operational carbon encompasses emissions produced during a building's use phase — primarily from heating, cooling, lighting, and appliances. While renewable energy can theoretically reduce operational carbon to zero, it currently represents about 28% of global emissions. Our calculator evaluates how different intervention strategies affect long-term operational efficiency over the building's expected lifespan.

When should I renovate instead of rebuilding?

Renovation is typically preferable when the existing structure has substantial reusable material (high structural integrity), when operational improvements can be achieved through targeted upgrades (insulation, windows, systems), and when the building has a remaining lifespan of 30+ years. Our tool quantifies these trade-offs by comparing whole-life carbon across scenarios, accounting for material reuse rates, operational efficiency gains, climate context, and building lifespan.

How accurate are the results?

This calculator provides indicative results based on research-backed assumptions and industry-standard LCA methodologies. Accuracy depends on input quality and project-specific factors not captured by simplified models. Results should be used for preliminary decision-making and comparative analysis. For regulatory compliance or investment decisions, we recommend conducting a detailed project-specific Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) according to EN 15978 or ISO 14040/14044 standards.

Who should use this tool?

This tool is designed for architects, engineers, sustainability consultants, property developers, facility managers, and policymakers evaluating building intervention strategies. It's particularly useful during early-stage feasibility studies, strategic planning, and stakeholder communication. The framework supports evidence-based decision-making aligned with Net Zero 2050 targets and circular economy principles in the built environment.

What data do I need to use the calculator?

You'll need basic building parameters: gross floor area (m²), expected lifespan post-intervention (years), structural material type (concrete, steel, timber, masonry, or mixed), climate zone (cold, temperate, warm, or hot), embodied energy factor (typical range: 200–800 kgCO₂/m²), and operational energy consumption (10–500 kWh/m²/yr). The tool will calculate and compare all seven scenarios automatically (Light, Medium, Deep, Deep+Demo Renovation vs Code-Compliant, High-Performance, Low-Carbon New Build).
